Katelyn Welsh's Tips for Writer's Block

Got Writer's Block?

Beat it with these tips.

Estimated time: 30 minutes to 2 hours
Awesome rating: you're 100% awesome

Got the Monday blues? Not feeling inspired? Just can't start? Try a few of thesemtips to get your creative juices flowing.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Transportation
  • Paper
  • Pen
  • Internet
  • Computer
  • Favorate cup of tea or coffee

  1. Change your scenery or environment to freshen things up.

    Fresh perspectives and new ideas often come when you switch up your routine. The change can be small or large. You can go to the park or you can take a trip somewhere you’ve never been before.

    It doesn't matter where. Just go!.

  2. Try word association to get the ideas flowing.

    This is a brain warm up. It gets ideas limbered up and moving. Go to your dictionary and randomly choose a word. Then, write down words you associate with it. Next, try it with the subject you seem to have hit a wall with.

  3. Write about your hobbies and interests.

    If you’re stuck or uninspired, writing about something you know about and love can help bring inspiration. You can write within a purely fictional context or write a vivid account of your latest adventure.

  4. Socialize and network to get ideas.

    Take the night off and hang out with your pals. Often, the best ideas come when you least expect it. So, relax, have some fun and let the ideas come to you.

  5. Learn about your favorite writers and their writing habits.

    Maybe what works for others will work for you. Research your favorite writers and their writing habits. If their habits don’t work for you, researching your favorite writer will at least provide some intrigue and possibly some inspiration.
